# Build Math 

This page is here for one reason:
to show the *scale* of creativity you get from a structured, competitive build system.

Masked Wrestlers uses a **7-piece loadout**:
1) Mask
2) Patch: Trait
3) Patch: Moves
4) Patch: Power
5) Boots
6) Tights
7) Top
8) Cape

(That’s 1 Mask + 3 Patch slots + 4 Ring Gear slots.)

Your progression runs **Level 1–1000**, and access to slots can be milestone-gated for balance.

---

## A concrete example catalog (v1 scale)
A realistic v1 catalog can look like:

### Patches (60 total)
- 10 Common Trait
- 10 Rare Trait
- 10 Rare Moves
- 10 Legendary Trait
- 10 Legendary Moves
- 10 Legendary Power

= **60 Patches**

### Ring Gear (80 total)
- 20 Boots
- 20 Tights
- 20 Top
- 20 Cape

= **80 Ring Gear**

Total example components: **140 items**.

---

## Ring Gear combinations alone (per Mask)
With 20 items per gear slot:

- Boots: 20 choices  
- Tights: 20 choices  
- Top: 20 choices  
- Cape: 20 choices  

That’s:

**20 × 20 × 20 × 20 = 160,000** Ring Gear looks/builds

And that’s before adding any Patch skills.

## The real takeaway (why this matters)
This scale creates three powerful player outcomes:

### 1) You can specialize
You can build toward a style that fits your hands:
pressure, control, defense, aerial/mixups, timing mastery.

### 2) The meta stays alive
A structured loadout system creates theorycrafting, matchup prep, and adaptation.

### 3) Ranked can stay fair
Even with huge variety, fairness stays protected when the system enforces:
- caps
- tradeoffs
- counterplay
- tier/slot/level rules
- anti-exploit protections

That’s the goal:
**tons of expression, zero pay-to-win.**
